Australia

Kangaroos are indigenous to Australia, Tasmania, and nearby islands. They thrive in diverse environments, including forests, woodlands, plains, savannas, and deserts. Their adaptability has led to a population surge to around 40-50 million. Normally, kangaroos reside in small groups referred to as troops or herds, often consisting of 50 or more individuals. The various kangaroo species inhabit distinct regions of Australia, with the red kangaroo boasting the broadest range. Like camels, they can endure periods without consuming water.

Asia

Red pandas inhabit the Himalayas and other high mountains, where they live in forests with bamboo and trees. They range from central China to northern Myanmar and Nepal. They prefer high altitudes and temperate temperatures. Almost 50% of the red panda's habitat is in the Eastern Himalayas. They spend most of their time in trees. Their semi-retractable claws help them move easily from branch to branch. They also wrap their long, bushy tails around themselves for warmth during the winter months.

Our Vision

At Continent Trek, we believe in the transformative power of exploration and the beauty that lies in every corner of our diverse world. Our vision is to inspire wanderlust and appreciation for the remarkable landscapes, cultures, and experiences that each continent has to offer.

Our Mission

Our mission is to be the ultimate guide for wanderers seeking inspiration for their next adventure. We curate and share stories, breathtaking images, and practical information about the most beautiful places on Earth. Through our platform, we aim to foster a sense of curiosity, connection, and appreciation for the incredible diversity that makes our world truly extraordinary.
